Dadasaheb Phalke

Father of Indian Cinema

Personal Info:-

    Full name: Dhundiraj Govind Phalke.
    Born: 30th April 1870, Trimbak, Maharashtra, India.
    Death: 16th February 1944, Nashik, Maharashtra, India.
    Spouse: Saraswatibai Phalke.

His life:-

    1885: Joined J. J. School of Arts, Mumbai, India. Developed interests in photography, lithography, dramatics. Worked as a painter, theritical set designer with Ravi Varma and got influenced by him.
    1908: Established Phalke's Art Printing and Engraving Works.
    1910: Saw his First silent film Life of Christ and got inspried to make an Indian one.
    1912: Went to London to learn film making from Cecil Hepworth.
    1913: Released his first Successful film "Raja Harishchandra" based on Indian Mythology, which was scripted, directed, produced and distributed by himself and it was a huge milestone in indian cinematic history. Released another film "Bhasmasur Mohini" introducing a female lead in a time when professional acting was a taboo for women.
    1917: Established Hindustan Film Company and produced several films.
    1969: For lifetime contribution to Indian Cinema an award was instituted by the government of India called Dadasaheb Phalke Award.
